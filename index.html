Another Love and Fire Metaphor

Written September 1, 2024

I hate that I'm still writing love poems
with metaphors for fire but I'd burn holes down to my bones
in the exact shape of your hands if you said you wanted
to touch me somewhere no one else had

I told myself I would stop writing these
when I said goodbye to the fallen Angel
who burned when he touched something holy
but I can't help but associate love with burn scars
because you never unlearn your first; semantic networks
are not easily rewired - trust me I would know

I'm getting a Leana Firestone tattoo because I want
a Foreverever reminder of the teenage girl
I'll apparently never stop being - I can't forget
the girl who was so scared of being forgotten that she remembered
the birthday of the boy who told her their expiration date

She deserves better than that and I learned the hard way
sometimes the only person who will give you what you deserve
is you

So I'll hold her memory in my mind and the ink
on my pages and etched into the skin of my arm
and she'll know what it feels like to be remembered
while I learn what it feels like to be loved - I'll do it
whether you're here or not so the question isn't

"Do you love me?"
it's
"Will you stay while I learn to?"
